My thesis: Parameter Fitting a Multi-scale Model of Biofilm Morphology using a Parallelized Multi-objective Genetic Algorithm

Summary

Dr. Nicholas Flann is my major professor. His research group has developed a biochemical and biomechanical model of biofilm wrinkling. Currently the model is programmed in Java. Our associates are working on implementing it in C++. A biofilm is a colony of bacteria glued to each other and to a surface. Sometimes localized regions of the colony die which results in wrinkling and folding. This creates liquid channels in the colony allowing for the secretion of waste and the dispersal of nutrients.

I've made significant improvements to this model including speeding it up over 10x, adding realistic cell growth and death, and increasing the sensitivity of the model to different types and rates of wrinkling. The bulk of my time has gone to programming a genetic algorithm and feature extraction program (also in Java), which fits the model parameters. My system compares simulated output to experimental data (of real biological biofilms) on the basis of features extracted from each. It runs many simulations in parallel in a local cluster and uses a multi-objective genetic algorithm to determine which sets of parameters results in wrinkling most closly matching the experimental data. The algorithm uses the experimental data to fit the model both spatially and temporally.
